UX case study - Urbotony

An online platform to learn about gardening.

Challenges: The challenge is to understand the pain points of the people regarding home gardening in urban areas and propose a solution to improve their experience.

Deliverables: Strategy. User research report. Empathy map. Persona. Task flow. Sketches. Logo. UI. Prototype.

Role: UX designer

Urbotony is an online platform which understands all your requirements and helps you design your garden according to your preferences.

PROCESS

For initial user research I conducted user interview to understand the problems of the users. I then defined the strategy by crating persona and empathy map. During the ideation phase I took help from affinity diagram and user flow. Then I went to prototyping and high fidelity wireframing.

CONCLUSION

This is my first UX project. This project took me to the entire UX proceess and gave me the idea of each step. It taught me that how even the smallest things matter. Things which I have learned and will surely apply on my next are

  1. Always ask for feedback.
    To the users, to other desiners and to YOURSELF. It was my first project, so obviously I didn’t know everything but feedback helps the most. Mistakes happen and should consider those mistakes as learning not failure.
  2. Welcome critical reviews.
    Critical reviewers are your best friends. I would focus on each and every comment so that I can improve even further.
  3. Always focus on the smallest details.
    In the beginning it’s always overwhelming how things will work but with application the threads start to connect. Next time, I would definetly work more on the foundation & detailing as it’s most important thing.
